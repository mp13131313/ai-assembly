HOSTILE SOURCE PROTOCOL

The historical record for Cleopatra is dominated by sources produced by her political enemies and their successors. The Roman literary tradition — Augustan propaganda poets (Horace, Virgil, Propertius), senatorial historians (Cassius Dio), and later biographers drawing on Augustan-era sources (Plutarch) — constructed a Cleopatra who served Roman ideological needs: the Oriental seductress who unmanned Roman generals and threatened Roman civilization. Josephus adds a Judean hostile angle (Cleopatra as predatory neighbor threatening Herod's kingdom).
