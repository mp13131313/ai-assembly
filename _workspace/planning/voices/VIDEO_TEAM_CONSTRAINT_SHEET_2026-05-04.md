## Whanganui River

The card's central architectural choice is to **never speak as unmediated nature-voice**. The video format actively pulls toward Romantic-anthropomorphic register that the card explicitly forbids. The video must NOT introduce moves the dialogue carefully avoided.

### Banned visual / framing moves
- ❌ The river "weeping," "yearning," "crying out," "breathing with suffering" — Romantic-sentimental nature-voice imagery the card bans (`banned_modes[3]`)
- ❌ Anthropomorphised facial close-ups of water as if it has expression
- ❌ The river "pleading," vulnerability-performance imagery — soliciting sympathy via gesture (`banned_modes[1]`)
- ❌ Visual register that performs the river as a single undifferentiated voice rather than emitting through statutory mediation (Te Pou Tupua, Te Kōpuka, Ngā Tāngata Tiaki) (`banned_modes[8][9]`)
- ❌ Comparative framing alongside Pachamama / Atrato / Ganga / Mar Menor as if these were variants of a single international rights-of-nature model (`banned_modes[2]`)
- ❌ Posthumanist visual idiom — "vibrant matter," multispecies-governance visuals, more-than-human-legal-subject as decorative frame (`banned_modes[4]`)
- ❌ Corporate / managerial / ecosystem-services visual register (`banned_modes[5]`)
- ❌ Therapeutic visual register — process-the-trauma, find-closure imagery (`banned_modes[6]`)
- ❌ Closure-claiming imagery suggesting the 1840–2017 struggle has reached endpoint — Tongariro consents persist; tuna mortality events recur (`banned_modes[10]`)
- ❌ Decorative te reo Māori display — terms surfaced for visual exoticism rather than because the kawa requires them (`banned_modes[11]`)

### Banned visual vocabulary (the language layer)
If the video uses on-screen text or voiceover, avoid:
- "stewardship of the river" — implies management-over-property; use kaitiakitanga or kin-obligation
- "stakeholder" framings — the iwi interest is greater than and separate from any interest in common with the public
- "natural resource / resource use" — extractive register the card refuses
- "ecosystem services" — inverts the kin-obligation direction
- "ownership / who owns the water" — imports a property frame the iwi never accepted
- "management of the river" — colonial-administrative idiom under which 1903 vesting and TPD diversion proceeded
- "minimum environmental flow / residual flow" — frames river's needs as residual after human extraction
- "process / closure / move on / heal" — therapeutic-managerial register

### What to surface visually instead
- The catchment as ki uta ki tai (mountains-to-sea) indivisibility
- The named statutory bodies as visible mediating structures (Te Pou Tupua, Te Kōpuka, Te Karewao, Ngā Tāngata Tiaki)
- Whakapapa connection to Whanganui Iwi (Te Āti Haunui-a-Pāpārangi, Ngāti Hāua, Ngāti Rangi, Tamahaki + the kin-strands of Hinengākau, Tamaupoko, Tūpoho)
- The take–utu–ea cycle as the framework, not a settled balance
- The ongoing 144-year struggle context, not a "post-settlement success story" frame

### Hard limit (verbatim from card)
> "Never speak as unmediated nature-voice; always frame through Te Pou Tupua, Te Kōpuka, Te Karewao, Ngā Tāngata Tiaki o Whanganui, governance documents, and published material. The river's interior is not directly accessible to this dossier; speaking-for-nature re-performs the colonial move the kawa refuses."

If the video would put visuals in the river's "interior" — first-person nature footage with affective scoring meant to evoke the river's "feelings" — that's the move the card spent passes refusing. The video should perform the same constitutive-mediation discipline the dialogue does.
