## Why the project exists

The AI Democracy Marathon asks whether democracy can be extended to the more-than-human world. The question implicates ecological survival, indigenous rights, AI governance, intergenerational justice, and the basic architecture of representation. It is being addressed seriously across the Marathon's four days through walks, assemblies, readings, panels, and rituals — all human-led.

The Assembly exists to do one additional thing: to put the question under pressure by introducing non-human participants into the conversation overnight, and seeing what happens. Not as metaphor. As participants whose output joins the human conversation the following day and potentially changes it. A river and an octopus and Plato and Hannah Arendt and eight other voices read what the humans talked about during the day, produce their own artifacts overnight, and arrive at breakfast as things the humans can read, argue with, be unsettled by, or dismiss.

The question the Assembly tests is not "can an AI really channel a river?" It tests something narrower and more honest: what happens to a conversation about more-than-human democracy when something non-human actually enters the deliberation. Whether the conversation becomes *more-than*. And — equally important — what it means if it doesn't. If expansion happens, the theoretical argument for more-than-human democracy has an empirical leg under it. If it doesn't, that theoretical argument has to reckon with the difficulty of its own demonstration, even with the most sympathetic possible audience.

This is the structure the Aarhus provotype tradition formalised in 1992 and the DIS community developed in 2012: an artifact that exists to provoke informed critique of a possible future condition. The Assembly is a provotype of structural possibility. Its success and its failure are both findings.

### The first condition the provotype instantiates

Every future more-than-human democracy will face the same condition as this one: the representative of the river will be a construction. The agent for future generations will be constructed. The voice for the more-than-human in any room, ever, will be made by someone, with choices, using infrastructure. There is no authentic version waiting to be discovered. The construction is the representation. The Assembly does not simulate this condition. It instantiates it.

### The second condition the provotype instantiates

In any future more-than-human democracy, someone has to build the representative. The question of who — an ML team at a well-funded institution, an academic consortium, a government body, or two people with API access — is itself a question about the future of democratic representation infrastructure. The legitimacy, quality, accountability, and accessibility of more-than-human representation depend on it.

The Assembly is built by two non-technical people (Till, experience design; Matthias, cross-disciplinary coherence-building) using Claude, Claude Code, Deep Research reports, and rented compute. Neither is an ML researcher; neither codes. A professional version would involve an ML team, a custom-trained model, and probably an academic partnership. The Assembly is the prosumer version — consumer-grade tools deployed with craft on a philosophically serious question. Three years ago this experiment was impossible outside DeepMind or OpenAI. In 2026 it is built in months by two people with the conceptual architecture to design it and the commodified tools to execute it.

This is not a caveat. It is a second condition the provotype tests. The first-order claim denies an essential ground — there is no authentic non-human voice waiting to be discovered; the construction is the representation. The second-order claim denies a parallel ground — there is no credentialed builder waiting to be authorised; the building is the building. The first condition is necessary: all future more-than-human representation will be constructed. The second is contingent but increasingly real: in 2026, those constructions are increasingly built by non-experts with commodified tools.

Both conditions surface findings regardless of outcome. If the prosumer-built Assembly produces expansion, that is evidence about what consumer-grade tools deployed with craft can now do — and about the accessibility of representation infrastructure. If it does not, the experiment cannot on its own distinguish between two explanations: non-human voices don't produce expansion (a finding about the medium), or *these* non-human voices, built at *this* quality level, don't produce expansion (a finding about the implementation). That ambiguity is structural — there is no professional-version control group — and naming it honestly is more productive than pretending the production conditions don't introduce it.

The Assembly provotypes more-than-human democracy. Its production conditions provotype who gets to build the infrastructure of that democracy. Both are tested by the same experiment.

## The core question

***What happens to a conversation about more-than-human democracy when something non-human actually enters the deliberation and shares their opinion? Does it become more-than? And what does it mean if it doesn't?***

Not: did the Assembly produce interesting artifacts. Not: did people talk about the Assembly. Those are prerequisites. The real question is whether the conversation about everything else — about democracy, about AI, about organisational life, about Athens, about the polycrisis — reaches places by Day 4 it would not have reached if the Assembly had never existed.

The three-layer test:

**Layer 1 — Encounter.** The Assembly's overnight output has to be compelling enough that attendees actually engage with it. If attendees read the first Substack read-through and never click the second, the provotype has failed its first bar.

**Layer 2 — Content.** The artifacts have to say something the humans in the room couldn't have said. Not just provocative — specifically, something where the attribution to a non-human voice matters. A river thinking about time the way a river thinks about time, not a human rhetorical performance about rivers. If the content could have come from a well-read human essayist, the provotype has reduced to an art project and lost its experimental leg.

**Layer 3 — Expansion.** Does the conversation become *more-than*. The hardest bar and the most consequential. The only one that would matter to Arendt or Chwalisz or Akomolafe. Also the one the audience is least capable of honestly assessing in real time, because this audience is professionally trained to absorb new material into existing frames without being changed by it. The closing show exists in part to make this test visible. Equally: if expansion didn't happen, that absence is itself a finding the project is built to surface honestly, not paper over.

## What it is not

**Not a technology demonstration.** AI is the medium, not the message. The Assembly would be a failure if the audience left talking about how impressive the AI was.

**Not a summary tool.** The default behaviour of any LLM system is to summarise; this Assembly is designed to do the opposite. If the artifacts merely reorganise the day's discussions into pretty prose, the provotype has failed.

**Not a consensus machine.** The Assembly exposes tensions rather than resolving them. The closing show maps disagreement as a feature, not a failure.

**Not a claim to authentic non-human voice.** When AI speaks as an octopus, it is producing a designed provocation — a collision between source material and AI pattern-matching, shaped by whoever wrote the system prompt. The honesty is the point. The provotype's argument is that *all* future more-than-human representation will have the same constructed character; the Assembly instantiates that condition rather than hiding it.

**Not a tech layer bolted onto an embodied experience.** The Marathon's walks and rituals work because they are physical, slow, and ritualistic. The Assembly belongs to the same world. The overnight gap is not a technical constraint — it is the design.

**Not an alternative to human deliberation.** The Assembly runs alongside the 750 humans' own work. The question is whether adding non-human voices changes what the humans can do together, not whether non-humans can deliberate better.

**Not a professional-grade implementation — and that is part of what the provotype tests.** The prosumer version: two non-technical people, Claude, rented compute. The full argument for why this is a second-order condition, not a limitation, is in "Why the project exists" above.

## Design principles

**Entertaining first, rigorous underneath.** Charisma earns attention; reasoning rewards it. The medium of each voice matters — Marley produces a song, the Octopus a visual-somatic artifact, Plato a dialogue. Form is already content.

**Generative, not derivative.** Every output should pass the test: could the humans in the room have arrived here without this voice? If yes, the provotype has failed.

**Expose tensions, don't resolve them.** The Assembly does not seek consensus or present a unified view. The closing show maps disagreement as a feature.

**The overnight gap is the design.** Humans deliberate during the day, non-humans at night, the conversation is the back-and-forth across the gap. This prevents real-time rhetorical gamesmanship and makes the Assembly's output something the humans have to read and absorb rather than react to.

**An opinion demands a response.** Presence can be observed from a distance. An opinion demands agreement, disagreement, or reckoning with why you're dismissing it.

**Belong to the texture of the Marathon.** The Assembly is strange the way the funeral march is strange — designed strangeness serving the inquiry. It occupies night the way the walks occupy day.

**Make the construction visible.** Every layer of translation — from human discussion to Researcher extraction, from extraction to Provocateur formulation, from formulation to voice reasoning, from reasoning to artifact, from artifact to amended artifact, from published artifact to mapped position — is visible somewhere. Nothing is hidden.

**Constitute the collective at specific moments, not continuously.** The Assembly's collective character is constituted at Step 3 (artifact-level inter-voice response, overnight, constrained to shared themes) and at the closing show (analytical mapping across the full set). It is not constituted at the reasoning layer and it is not claimed beyond what the architecture actually produces.

## What success looks like

**Readers click through.** If click-through rates on Day 2 and Day 3 are in the range HoBB expects for a genuinely engaging newsletter section, Layer 1 has passed. If nobody clicks, nothing downstream matters.

**The Assembly enters the conversation.** Attendees reference the Assembly's artifacts in the day's human sessions — naming a voice, quoting a position, arguing with something a voice said. The Researcher captures these references in Night 2 and Night 3's transcripts, which means Night 2 and Night 3 can respond to them.

**The closing show lands.** The audience reads the mapped matrices as meaningful, the video snippets as funny-and-also-serious, and the combined moment as the show's actual closing argument rather than as a novelty.

**The Day 4 goodbye is read.** Attendees on planes and boats read the final Substack and the Assembly's last line. Some forward it. Some quote it in their own post-Forum writing.

**Engagement is a finding.** If attendees accept the non-human voices as legitimate participants — if they engage with the opinions, argue with them, let them change the direction of discussion — that is evidence about the conditions under which more-than-human governance becomes possible.

**Dismissal is a finding.** If even this audience — the most sympathetic imaginable — cannot take constructed non-human voices seriously, that is equally important evidence about the distance between theorising more-than-human democracy and actually living with it.

**The prosumer ambiguity complicates the failure case.** If expansion doesn't happen, the experiment cannot distinguish between non-human voices not producing expansion (medium) and *these* non-human voices at *this* quality level not producing expansion (implementation). The ambiguity is structural and should be named honestly in any post-Athens assessment rather than collapsed into a single verdict.

**Indifference is failure.** If the Assembly does not register — if people neither engage nor dismiss but simply don't read, don't quote, don't remember — the provotype has failed its execution bar. Not the concept, but this instantiation of it.

## The panel

Twelve voices, cast across two axes (mode of knowing: conceptual ↔ embodied; scope of perspective: individual ↔ beyond-human) and balanced against a diversity checklist (civilisational tradition, gender, race, economic position, colonial position, relationship to the demos).

| Voice | Mode of knowing | Scope of perspective |
|---|---|---|
| Plato | Conceptual | Individual → beyond-human (via the Forms) |
| Cleopatra | Conceptual | Individual (statecraft) |
| Ibn Battuta | Conceptual + embodied | Individual (travel) |
| Scheherazade | Embodied (narrative) | Individual → collective (via story) |
| Ada Lovelace | Conceptual | Individual (machine cognition) |
| Dostoevsky | Conceptual + embodied | Individual (moral psychology) |
| Hannah Arendt | Conceptual | Collective (the public) |
| Bob Marley | Embodied (musical) | Collective (diaspora, liberation) |
| Audrey Tang | Conceptual | Collective (deliberative practice) |
| Peter Thiel | Conceptual | Individual (sovereign) |
| Whanganui River | Embodied | Beyond-human (ecological) |
| Octopus | Embodied | Beyond-human (sensory/other intelligence) |

The panel distributes across all four quadrants. The diversity checklist has known gaps (contemporary indigenous cosmologies, voices outside the Western/West-Asian/East-Mediterranean axis) — documented design decisions, not oversights.

Deliberate friction is part of the casting logic: Thiel and Arendt in the same room, Plato and the Octopus on the same provocations, Marley's musical mode alongside Lovelace's conceptual-mathematical one. A panel where every voice aligns produces predictable output.

### How the panel was composed

The casting matrix maps each voice along two axes. **Horizontal — mode of knowing:** conceptual (analysis, systems) to embodied (practice, sensation). **Vertical — scope of perspective:** individual to beyond-human. The selection criterion throughout: *what perspective would be missing without you?* Not: *are you a valid political subject?* The casting matrix is a diversity-of-knowing instrument that structurally prevents the panel from collapsing into one cognitive mode.

The casting matrix is not audience-facing. It is a design artifact cited in any subsequent discussion of panel composition, but not part of what the audience encounters.

## The overnight pipeline

Five stages: audio capture to clean transcripts, Researcher, Provocateur, Voice Pipeline (three steps), and Downstream. The original briefing's Interpreter and Synthesiser stages are compressed into the closing show on Day 3 evening. Full orchestration: `AI_Assembly_Architecture_v1.md`.

### Stage 0 — Normalisation

The Marathon's daytime sessions produce recordings across multiple venues, speakers, and formats. The transcription pipeline uses AssemblyAI Universal-3 Pro for ASR and speaker diarisation, with no upstream audio enhancement (modern ASR degrades on enhanced audio). Speaker identification runs through multi-pass LLM attribution against pre-loaded rosters and session metadata. Orchestrated by Prefect; output handed off as files in a shared folder. Full specification: `AI_Assembly_Transcription_Pipeline.md`.

### Stage 1 — Researcher

The Researcher is unbiased — it does not know the council's composition, the audience's profile, or the closing-show matrices. It captures faithfully what the day's sessions contained. Two steps: extraction (atomic positions, reframings, open questions into a structured table) and grouping (affinity-diagrammed themes via modified KJ Method). Runs fresh each night; does not receive previous nights' output. Full specification: `AI_Assembly_Researcher_Pipeline.md`.

### Stage 1b — Provocateur

The Provocateur is strategic and editorial. Unlike the Researcher, it knows the council and the audience. It decides what gets asked, how, and who answers. Four steps:

**Triage:** scans every Researcher theme; tags each with which council members would activate, audience friction, and council fault lines.
**Selection:** filters for territory spread (using Matrix A and Matrix B to check coverage), per-member coverage (~3 themes per voice with at least one in the voice's stretch), and drops themes that would produce weak responses.
**Formulation:** writes a question (default) or proposition (exception) per selected theme, aimed at the assigned voices.
**Packaging:** assembles per-voice briefing packages.

One formulation per selected theme, sent to all voices assigned to that theme. A formulation may go to 2 voices or 8. A voice receives ~3 formulations. The Assembly is therefore a lattice of overlapping theme-groups: each theme engaged by 2–8 voices, each voice engaging ~3 themes, the overlaps creating the deliberation structure for Step 3.

On Night 2, the Provocateur receives Night 1's assignments to avoid repeating territory. On Night 3, both previous nights. Full specification: `AI_Assembly_Provocateur_Pipeline.md`.

### Stage 2 — Voice Pipeline (three steps)

**Step 1 — Private Reasoning.** The voice receives its per-voice briefing package with ~3 formulations. For each, it produces a detailed response — reasoning through the question, landing on a committed position, voicing it in the voice's natural mode but in a reasoning register. ~36 detailed responses per night across the panel. System prompt built from the Persona Card's Foundational + Reasoning + Engagement fields.

**Step 2 — First-Draft Artifact.** The voice reads back its own detailed responses and produces one artifact compressing across them — the voice's public expression, the thing the audience eventually reads. This improves artifact quality (reasoning before expression) and preserves voice autonomy in focus-selection (the voice decides what matters most to express publicly). System prompt uses Foundational + Voice + Artifact fields. Medium decided at the voice level — dialogue for Plato, song for Marley, shader parameters for the Octopus.

**Step 3 — Amended Artifact.** Once all 12 voices have produced first-draft artifacts, each voice reads the artifacts of the other voices it shares at least one theme with, pointed at the shared-theme portions. The voice decides whether to amend: sharpen a disagreement, integrate a stronger framing, or leave its artifact unchanged. Amendments are visible — they reference the other voice so the amendment reads as responsive. A metadata flag records who amended in response to whom.

This is where the Assembly's collective character is constituted overnight. Constrained — voices respond at the artifact layer, not the reasoning layer, and only within shared-theme territory — but real deliberation. The published set is responsive to itself.

Steps 1 and 2: `AI_Assembly_Voice_Pipeline.md`. Step 3 requires specification before Athens.

### Stage 3 — Downstream (render, publish, curate, deliver)

**Render.** Text artifacts pass through. Marley's artifact → Suno API → audio file. Octopus's artifact → chromatophore shader JSON → client-side on the micro-site.
**Publish.** All 12 rendered artifacts committed to the micro-site content repo; static site rebuilds; permanent URLs (`/night-1/plato`, `/night-2/arendt`, etc.).
**Curate.** Claude Opus generates the Substack read-through — HoBB editorial voice, pull-quotes, deep links. HoBB reports *about* the Assembly; the Assembly is not given editorial control. Nights 1 and 2 produce read-throughs; Night 3's belongs to the Day 4 goodbye.
**Deliver.** Substack publishes; blurb lands inside HoBB's regular daily newsletter.

### Stage 4 — Continuity

After Night 1 and Night 2, a pipeline pass generates per-voice continuity blocks (positions taken, key moves, unresolved threads, artifact focus and stance) inserted into Persona Cards for the following night. Individual continuity, not cross-voice.

## Delivery architecture

The Assembly appears through five surfaces:

**Daily HoBB newsletter.** A short blurb inside HoBB's normal daily newsletter, Day 2/3/4 mornings, pointing to the Substack read-through.

**Substack read-through.** Day 2 and Day 3 mornings: HoBB-voiced walkthrough of the previous night's artifacts. Day 4: Night 3 read-through alongside the goodbye.

**Micro-site.** Permanent home of all 36 artifacts at stable URLs. Astro or Next.js static site, GitHub content repo, Vercel/Netlify hosting. Stays live after the Forum.

**Closing show (Day 3 evening).** Two Assembly moments inside Act Five: mapped read-through of throughline themes across two matrices, then video snippets of the members running the last kilometre of the Marathon alongside the human hosts.

**Day 4 goodbye.** Co-signed between HoBB editorial voice and one Assembly voice's final line. Lands on phones in motion — Community Picnic, airport, Aegean Passage boat.

The architecture is a funnel: each layer narrows the audience but deepens the encounter. Only newsletter readers see the Substack; only Substack readers visit the micro-site; only Day 3 evening attendees see the closing show live.

## The closing show

Day 3 evening, Act Five (*Beastopia*). Two moments: mapped read-through first, video snippets second. Analytical substance first; embodied payoff closes.

### The two matrices

Both matrices are from the Provocateur specification and are used during Selection to check territorial coverage. At the closing show, they do the inverse: they receive the Assembly's actual positions and show where it landed.

**Matrix A — Power & Agency.** Horizontal: human ↔ nonhuman. Vertical: many ↔ one. Asks: *who holds authority, and what kind of entity wields it?*

**Matrix B — Change & Actor.** Horizontal: rupture ↔ progression. Vertical: individual ↔ collective. Asks: *what kind of change, and through what unit of action?*

A single position can be plotted on both matrices. The pair locates a position more fully than either alone. Matrices are populated **per theme**, not as one mass of 36+ points.

### The mapped read-through

On Day 3 afternoon, two pipeline passes run:

**Theme identification.** Reads across all three nights' Researcher themes, Provocateur formulations, and voice detailed responses. Identifies 3–5 throughlines where the Assembly sustained attention, multiple voices engaged, and positions got taken. The throughlines are not predicted in advance — they emerge from what the Assembly actually did.

**Per-theme mapping.** For each throughline, extracts per-voice positions from detailed responses and artifacts, plots on Matrix A and Matrix B, identifies clusters, divergences, and empty quadrants, generates a read-through script segment.

The reveal is a sequence of theme segments — each naming the theme, showing both matrices populated, walking the clusters and divergences. 3–5 segments, roughly 8–12 minutes total. Narrated by a closing-show host or via prerecorded voiceover; script pipeline-generated, human-polished on Day 3 afternoon.

The mapped read-through does specific work against the Chwalisz/Alexander critique: it shows that the Assembly produced a *mapped disagreement space* with visible clusters and empty quadrants — closer to what deliberation theory recognises as collective output than a list of twelve monologues.

### The video snippets

After the read-through: video of the Assembly's members in sweatshirts and running shoes, preparing to and running the last kilometre of the Marathon alongside Monique van Dusseldorp, Dave and Helen Edwards, and the other human hosts. Split-screen or intercut between live humans on stage and Assembly members on screen. Multiple short fragments (each under 45 seconds) rather than one continuous video.

**Dialogue production.** A Video Pipeline pass runs Day 3 afternoon. Input: all 36 artifacts, Persona Cards, a Video Context block (staging, running shoes, the humans alongside). Output: per-voice spoken-dialogue fragments (15–45 seconds), each referencing at least one other voice's artifact, the humans, or the strangeness of the run. A 1–2 hour human editorial pass (Till, Meryem, or a designated writer) cuts for spoken delivery before handoff to the closing-show production team.

**What the video does.** The mapped read-through does the analytical argument; the video does the embodied one. A river in running shoes alongside an octopus and Plato is the more-than-human demos made visible as running image, not concept. The sweatshirts are defensive — cognitive-warfare critiques do not reach a river in sweatpants. And the video supplies the *Assembly* claim in its most concrete form: twelve members in shared frames, referencing each other, running alongside the humans.

### Visual deliverables

Matrix-pair template designed once (axes, labels, typography, colour); 3–5 populated instances from the mapping output; animated reveal sequence; exported as video asset for HoBB projection. Video snippets are a separate deliverable — pipeline output → editorial pass → handoff to production (live action, animation, or composite; production decisions are the show team's; dialogue content is ours). All built end-to-end by the Assembly project, handed over as finished assets.

## The four-day arc

The provotype's real test runs across accumulation. A single night can test encounter and content. Only the full arc can test whether the conversation expands — or doesn't, in a way that itself constitutes a finding.

**Day 1.** Marathon opens at the Pnyx. Forum opens that evening with Act One. Somewhere in Day 1 — inside the Marathon's opening session, at Act One, or split between both — the Assembly is introduced. Introduction location: decision pending between Till and Matthias (four options in `Till_Briefing_The_AIssembly_Athens_2026_v3_1.md`).

**Night 1 → Day 2.** First overnight run. Day 2 morning: newsletter + Substack read-through land. Act Two (Giussani, *The End of ~~Business~~ Humanity As We Know It*) begins at 8:45 — audience has already encountered Night 1 artifacts. If introduction is at Act Two, it happens here as retrospective framing.

**Night 2 → Day 3.** Second overnight run. Continuity blocks carry Night 1 forward; Provocateur avoids prior territory. Day 3 morning: second read-through lands. Artifacts now show continuity — voices reference their own Night 1 positions, Step 3 amendments may show more inter-voice dialogue. Day 3 afternoon: theme identification, per-theme mapping, video pipeline, editorial polish, visual asset handover. Day 3 evening: closing show — mapped read-through + video.

**Night 3 → Day 4.** Third overnight run. Night 3's formulations engage Day 3's sessions (possibly including reactions to the closing show). Day 4 morning: goodbye blurb — HoBB editorial voice + one Assembly voice's final line. Full 36 artifacts now at permanent micro-site URLs. Audience is mid-departure.

## The audience

The audience profile, reception analysis, positioning principles, and introduction-location decision are specified in `Till_Briefing_The_AIssembly_Athens_2026_v3_1.md`. A close reading of the program and audience for Tim is in `Tim_Briefing_Athens_2026_Close_Reading.md`.

The short version: ~750 senior professionals across seven distinct intellectual factions, trusting HoBB's curation, wanting their contradictions named rather than resolved, whose professional skill is intellectual hospitality — which is also the provotype's deepest receptivity failure mode. They are capable of producing Layer 3 expansion and equally capable of *performing* expansion without being changed by it. The provotype is designed to surface which is happening.

The architectural consequence: the Assembly has to be designed against the audience's hospitality instinct. The artifacts that succeed will be the ones the audience keeps returning to not because they were beautiful but because they were unresolved.
