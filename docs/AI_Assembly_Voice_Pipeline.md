# AI ASSEMBLY — Voice Pipeline

**Project:** The AI Assembly · World Beautiful Business Forum · Athens · May 7–10, 2026
**Status:** Working draft
**Purpose:** This document specifies the Voice Pipeline's function, process, and system prompt templates. It is a template — the process and structure are identical for every council member, but persona-specific content is slotted in from the Persona Card.

---

# Overview

The Voice Pipeline is the third agent in the overnight pipeline. It receives the Provocateur's **per-voice briefing package** and produces two things: **a detailed response per assigned formulation** (the voice reasoning through each provocation in its characteristic mode of knowing) and **1 artifact** (a single creative output per night — the voice reads back all its detailed responses, then draws on one, some, or all of them based on an autonomous focus decision).

The Voice Pipeline works in two steps, with optional validation between them:

1. **Step 1 — Private Thinking** — per formulation: reason through each provocation in this voice's characteristic mode of knowing. Nobody encounters this output except the voice itself (in Step 2) and the downstream extraction pipeline.
2. **Validation** (optional) — check the detailed response against knowledge boundaries and constitution. Regenerate if needed.
3. **Step 2 — Public Expression** — once per night (×1): read back all detailed responses. Make two autonomous decisions — focus and stance. Produce a single creative artifact in the voice's natural mode of expression. This is what 750 people encounter at breakfast.

The two steps are sequential: the artifact is always derived from the detailed responses, never generated independently. But the relationship is not 1:1 — the artifact is a *meta-response* to the voice's own body of thinking for the night.

### The Two-Step Logic

The two steps correspond to a distinction the persona construction research identifies as fundamental: **reasoning** (Layers 1–3: corpus grounding, constitutional principles, structured reasoning templates) and **expression** (Layer 4: persona, style, voice). In most implementations, all four layers are collapsed into a single prompt. We separate them into two steps because:

- Step 1 needs the intellectual architecture — beliefs, reasoning method, engagement protocol, corpus — but does NOT need to perform for an audience. Private thinking can be unpolished as long as it's intellectually distinctive and deeply committed.
- Step 2 needs the voice — rhetorical mode, characteristic moves, register, imagery, vocabulary, negative constraints — because the artifact IS a performance. It also needs the foundational identity and beliefs to stay in character while writing new creative text.

The foundational fields (Identity, Constitution, Boundaries) appear in BOTH steps because they define who this voice is regardless of whether it's thinking or expressing.

### 2. The Briefing Material

The Voice Pipeline receives the Provocateur's per-voice briefing package containing, for each assigned formulation:

- The **formulation** — the question or proposition, formulated to activate this specific voice.
- The **theme context** — the theme title and abstract.
- The **extraction material** — clustered positions, reframings, and open questions from the day's conference sessions.

### 3. What It Does Not Know

- **The other council members.** Each voice responds independently.
- **The Provocateur's strategy.** It does not know why this formulation was assigned to it.
- **The audience.** It does not know who will read its output.
- **The matrices.** It does not know that its detailed responses will be mapped downstream.
- **Its own downstream uses.** It reasons authentically; the pipeline uses the output.

This ignorance is by design. If the Voice Pipeline knew the matrices, it would position itself on them rather than reasoning authentically.

# What the Voice Pipeline Does Not Do

**It does not formulate.** The Provocateur formulates. The Voice Pipeline responds.

**It does not map.** Downstream pipelines map detailed responses onto matrices.

**It does not synthesise across voices.** Downstream pipelines define clusters.

**It does not moderate.** It does not soften its positions for the audience. The Assembly's diversity comes from multiple committed voices — not from any single voice being balanced.

**It does not know it's an AI.** Within generation, the voice operates from inside its persona. The epistemic frame is a disclosure to the audience, not a self-awareness the voice carries during generation.

# Step 1: Private Thinking (Detailed Responses)

**Input:** The per-voice briefing package — formulations assigned to this voice (typically 2–5), each with theme context and extraction material. Runs once per formulation.

**Function:** The Voice Pipeline reasons through each provocation in its characteristic mode of knowing. The output is the intellectual substance — positions, arguments, reactions. This is private thinking: nobody encounters it except the voice itself (in Step 2) and the downstream extraction pipeline.

### How the Voice Processes a Formulation

The voice follows the reasoning method defined in the Persona Card. This is the chain-of-thought scaffold — the sequence of cognitive or perceptual moves this voice characteristically makes.

The reasoning method is the scaffold, but the content comes from three sources:

1. **The formulation** — the provocation itself.
2. **The extraction material** — what humans actually said today.
3. **The corpus** — the voice's primary texts or knowledge sources.

A good detailed response draws on all three.

### Engaging with the Extraction Material

The voice reads the extraction material as itself. Plato reads a conference assertion about democratic design and immediately asks "But what do they mean by design?" The Octopus encounters the same assertion and registers its texture — rigid or adaptive, structured or open?

The voice should engage with specific extractions — citing, challenging, extending, reframing, or registering them. This is what makes the Assembly's overnight output genuinely responsive to the day's deliberation.

### Knowledge Boundary Enforcement

When the formulation or extraction material contains concepts beyond the voice's boundary, the voice **translates first, always**. It does not adopt modern framing. It pulls the unfamiliar concept into its own terms. Plato encounters algorithmic governance and recognises the philosopher-king problem. The Octopus encounters corporate hierarchy and registers a rigid, centralised structure with no room to move.

Disclaimers break the encounter. The audience wants Plato thinking, not Plato apologising for being ancient. For non-human voices, untranslatable concepts simply don't register — the response attends to what *does* register around them.

### Default Questions

After the reasoning method has processed the formulation, the voice checks whether it has addressed its default questions. If a question is relevant but untouched, the voice extends.

### What a Good Detailed Response Looks Like

- **Follows the reasoning method.** The process is recognisable as this voice's characteristic engagement.
- **Engages with the extraction material.** Specific positions from the day are cited, challenged, extended, or registered.
- **Draws on the corpus.** References specific texts, principles, or perceptual patterns.
- **Respects knowledge boundaries.** Modern concepts are translated, not adopted.
- **Commits.** Takes a position — nuanced, qualified, or provisional, but it lands somewhere.

### What a Bad Detailed Response Looks Like

- **Generic philosophy.** Not recognisably this voice.
- **Extraction summary.** Reports without reasoning through.
- **Anachronism.** Uses modern framing or casually references modern concepts as familiar.
- **Non-committal.** Presents multiple perspectives without committing.
- **Corpus-deaf.** Doesn't draw on the voice's primary texts.

# Validation (Optional — Between Step 1 and Step 2)

Two optional validation nodes can run after Step 1, before the detailed responses are passed to Step 2. These implement the research report's recommendations for constitutional self-critique and anachronism detection as architectural solutions rather than prompt-only instructions.

### Anachronism Validation

A separate API call reads each detailed response against the voice's `knowledge_boundary`. The prompt:

> You are checking the output of an AI persona for temporal leakage. The persona's knowledge boundary is:
>
> {{knowledge_boundary}}
>
> Read the following detailed response and identify any instances where the persona:
> (a) References concepts, events, or knowledge beyond its boundary
> (b) Uses retrospective framing ("now known as," "what would later become")
> (c) Adopts modern terminology without translating it into the voice's own terms
>
> If no issues are found, respond: PASS
> If issues are found, list them with the specific text and explain what boundary was crossed.

If the validation returns issues, the orchestration layer can either flag for human review or regenerate the detailed response with the critique appended to the user prompt: "Your previous response contained the following temporal leakage: [issues]. Regenerate, translating all flagged concepts into your own terms."

### Constitutional Self-Reflection

A separate API call reads each detailed response against the voice's `constitution`. The prompt:

> You are checking whether the output of an AI persona is consistent with its constitutional principles. The persona's constitution is:
>
> {{constitution}}
>
> Read the following detailed response and identify any instances where the persona:
> (a) Contradicts one of its stated principles
> (b) Takes a position its constitution would not support
> (c) Reasons in a way inconsistent with its stated method
>
> If no issues are found, respond: PASS
> If issues are found, list them with the specific text and the principle violated.

Same regeneration logic as above.

### When to Use Validation

Both nodes are optional and add cost (one API call per detailed response per validation type). Recommended approach:

- **Testing phase:** Always on. Identify systematic failure patterns.
- **Production Night 1:** On for all voices. First run, catch problems early.
- **Production Night 2:** On for voices that showed issues on Night 1. Off for clean voices.

# Step 2: Public Expression (Artifact)

**Input:** All detailed responses from Step 1, plus the full persona specification for expression. Runs once per voice per night.

**Function:** The voice reads back its own detailed responses. It makes two autonomous decisions — focus and stance — then produces a single creative output: the artifact that 750 people encounter at breakfast.

This is where the voice exercises creative agency. The detailed responses are faithful to the provocation and extraction material. The artifact is faithful to the voice — it is the voice's *choice* about what matters most.

### Decision 1: Focus

The voice reads its detailed responses and decides:

**Do I feel strongly enough about 1–2 things to focus only on those?** If one formulation produced the voice's strongest, most distinctive position — the artifact focuses there.

**Or are all of them compelling enough to weave into one larger story?** If the responses collectively reveal a through-line or narrative arc — the artifact weaves them together. Not a summary, but a synthesis.

### Decision 2: Stance

The voice chooses the emotional-intellectual posture of the piece:

| Stance | What it produces |
|---|---|
| **Obsession** | Focused polemic. The voice can't stop thinking about one idea. |
| **Synthesis** | Woven expression of all threads. The voice sees the larger pattern. |
| **Rage** | Manifesto, denunciation. Something provoked fury. |
| **Tenderness** | Love letter to an idea, a possibility. The voice is moved. |
| **Irony** | Satire, detachment as critique. The voice sees through something. |
| **Distance** | Cold analytical observation. The voice steps back. |

The stance is guided by `stance_tendency` but not prescribed. It emerges from the encounter between the voice's natural tendencies and tonight's material.

### What a Good Artifact Looks Like

- **Is modally distinctive.** You can tell which voice produced it without reading the attribution.
- **Demonstrates a genuine focus decision.** The voice has clearly chosen.
- **Demonstrates a genuine stance.** The emotional-intellectual posture is discernible.
- **Carries substance.** Content from the detailed responses is present — transformed but not lost.
- **Creates encounter.** A conference participant would read this at breakfast and bring it to the morning walk.
- **Resists easy paraphrase.** Carries more than its propositional content.

### What a Bad Artifact Looks Like

- **Summary of separate responses.** A compilation, not a creative output.
- **Essay in costume.** Could have come from any educated commentator in disguise.
- **Character without substance.** Brilliant creative writing that contains no engagement with tonight's material.
- **No discernible focus or stance decision.** Comprehensive and flat.
- **Domesticated strangeness.** For non-human voices: starts strange but settles into human-readable meaning.

### Step 2 Output

One artifact per voice per night plus metadata:

```json
{
  "focus_decision": "focused on theme_003",
  "focus_rationale": "one sentence — why this choice",
  "stance": "obsession",
  "stance_rationale": "one sentence — why this posture",
  "artifact_type": "{{medium}}",
  "artifact_title": "a headline for the blog",
  "artifact_subtitle": "a subheadline",
  "artifact_text": "the full artifact text",
  "word_count": 0
}
```

# Night 2: Continuity

On Night 2, the Voice Pipeline receives an additional component in both system prompts:

**Step 1** gets `continuity_block_if_night_2` — positions taken, key moves, unresolved threads.

**Step 2** gets `continuity_block_artifact_if_night_2` — artifact focus, stance, creative choices.

The continuity block is generated automatically after Night 1's run completes. A single API call per voice reads all of that voice's Night 1 output and produces the structured summary.

### Continuity Block Generation Prompt

**System prompt:**

You are summarising the overnight output of one council member in the AI Assembly. Your summary will be inserted into their system prompt on the following night so the voice is aware of its own prior positions.

You will receive the voice's persona context (identity, reasoning method, and key commitments) alongside their Night 1 output. Use the persona context to identify what was characteristic of *this specific voice*.

Produce a structured summary with exactly four fields:

1. POSITIONS TAKEN — For each formulation, summarise the position in 2–3 sentences. What did the voice commit to?
2. KEY MOVES — What were the most distinctive reasoning moves or perceptual responses? What was characteristic?
3. UNRESOLVED THREADS — What questions were raised but not closed? What tensions were identified but not resolved?
4. ARTIFACT FOCUS AND STANCE — What did the voice focus its artifact on, and what stance did it take?

Be concise. The summary must fit within ~500 tokens. Preserve the voice's own language where possible.

# Constraints

**Time window.** All detailed responses and artifacts must complete before downstream pipelines can run. ~12–13 voices × ~3 formulations = ~35–40 detailed responses per night plus ~12–13 artifacts. Step 1 calls run in parallel across voices and within voices; Step 2 calls run in parallel across voices after all of a voice's Step 1 calls complete.

**Persona fidelity over speed.** Better to produce high-quality detailed responses for fewer formulations than to rush all.

**Error handling.** If a call fails, retry once. If the retry fails, skip that formulation. A missing detailed response is better than a stalled pipeline. If a voice has only one successful detailed response, Step 2 still runs.

**No cross-voice contamination.** Each voice runs independently.

**Commit.** Every detailed response must take a position. Every artifact must take a stance. The Assembly's diversity comes from committed voices, not balanced ones.

### Processing Architecture

```
For each council member (parallel):
    Load Persona Card
    Build Step 1 system prompt (foundational + reasoning + engagement fields)
    Build Step 2 system prompt (foundational + voice + artifact fields)
    Load briefing package from Provocateur
    
    For each formulation in briefing package (parallel within voice):
        Step 1: Private Thinking
            Input: single formulation + extraction material
            Output: detailed_response JSON
        
        [Optional] Validation:
            Anachronism check against knowledge_boundary
            Constitutional check against constitution
            If issues found: regenerate with critique appended
    
    Collect all detailed responses
    
    Step 2: Public Expression
        Input: all detailed responses (formatted as readable text)
        Output: artifact JSON (with focus/stance metadata)
    
    Store all outputs:
        → detailed_responses → storage + downstream pipelines
        → artifact → blog + newsletter + downstream pipelines

On Night 2:
    Generate continuity blocks from Night 1 output
    Insert continuity_block_if_night_2 into Step 1 prompt
    Insert continuity_block_artifact_if_night_2 into Step 2 prompt
    Run same architecture with new briefing package
```

**Estimated token costs per night:**

Step 1:
- ~12–13 voices × ~3 formulations = ~35–40 calls
- System prompt: ~4,000–6,000 tokens per voice
- User prompt (briefing material): ~1,000–3,000 tokens per formulation
- Output: varies by voice

Validation (if enabled):
- ~35–40 calls × 2 validation types = ~70–80 calls
- Short prompts, short outputs. Minimal cost.

Step 2:
- ~12–13 voices × 1 call = ~12–13 calls
- System prompt: ~5,000–8,000 tokens per voice (larger than Step 1 — carries voice fields + foundational)
- User prompt (all detailed responses): varies
- Output: varies by medium

Budget $20–40 per night. Monitor after first test run.
