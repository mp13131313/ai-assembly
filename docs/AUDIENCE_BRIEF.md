## Part 1 — The House of Beautiful Business: Organisation and Brand

### Origin and founders

The House of Beautiful Business (HoBB) was founded in 2017 by Tim Leberecht and Till Grusche, two former Silicon Valley marketing executives who met at frog design. The origin event was a rented guild house in Barcelona's Gothic Quarter during Mobile World Congress — a deliberate counter-event to the 100,000-person tech trade show happening outside. Leberecht had published *The Business Romantic* (HarperCollins, 2015, translated into nine languages) and delivered a TED talk on building human companies viewed over 1.5 million times.

The organisation operates through The Business Romantic Society GmbH, registered in Berlin, with a distributed team across Berlin, Istanbul, Barcelona, and Atlanta. Seed-funded by Porsche (through its digital company builder Forward31). Client roster: Accenture, BCG, Chanel, Deloitte, Google, Microsoft, SAP, Salesforce, Volkswagen Group, among others.

### Community and reach

A community of 50,000+ professionals with 24,000+ Substack subscribers. Pop-up gatherings in 30+ cities worldwide. Annual flagship festivals deliberately kept at 250–750 attendees.

### Intellectual framework

The manifesto declares that beautiful business is "not harsh but soft and tender, not rigid but fluid and flexible, not toxically positive but melancholic, not numbers-oriented but poetic, not efficient but sustainable, not 'human-centered' but humane." This is the core intellectual framework, not decorative language. HoBB explicitly positions beauty, mystery, and inefficiency as business virtues.

The current organising concept is the "life-centered economy" — an evolution from the earlier "humanising business" language that now encompasses all forms of life, not just human flourishing.

### Brand voice and tonal signature

The tone is poetic-intellectual, countercultural yet corporate-adjacent — closer to a European feuilleton or literary salon than a business publication. Tim Leberecht's writing blends personal confession with civilisational diagnosis, drawing on Continental philosophy, German Romanticism, Buddhist practice, and media theory.

Four features distinguish the HoBB-native voice as observed in the Athens program:

- **Comfort with paradox as rhetorical structure.** "Intelligence after Intelligence" paired with "How to Meet an Idiot." "The Ugliest Dinner Party in the World" in the Beauty Salon. The strikethrough on "Business" in Act Two's title.
- **Philosophical and poetic registers as primary** — not supplementary. Quarch on Plato is core content, not decoration.
- **Spiritual without devotional.** Temple of Reenchantment, Sacred Business — vocabulary borrowed without doctrinal commitment. Secular but reverent, modern but enchantable.
- **Aesthetic decisions as content.** Venue choice (music theatres and stock exchanges over convention centres), elimination of name tags, the running-shoe imagery — all carry information.

The distinguishing capacity is **seriousness without solemnity** — discussing civilisation collapse and then attending a philosophical speed-dating rave without dissonance. Outsiders fail either as corporate-keynote earnestness or as generic-radical pose without specificity.

### Positioning in the conference landscape

**Compared to Davos:** intimate rather than powerful, meaning-driven rather than deal-driven, arts-integrated rather than policy-focused. Yet HoBB has hosted pop-up events at Davos since 2023 from a venue called the "Museum of Belonging." The corporate constituency overlaps (SAP, Porsche, Google, Deloitte). After Davos 2026, Leberecht wrote about "alexithymia" — the inability to name one's emotions — as a diagnosis of elite gatherings.

**Compared to TED:** participatory rather than performative, messy rather than polished, question-driven rather than answer-driven. TED offers "ideas worth spreading" as finished products; HoBB offers "beautiful questions" as unfinished provocations. Leberecht is himself a three-time TED speaker; TED curator Bruno Giussani has spoken at HoBB.

**Compared to SXSW and Web Summit:** microscopic in scale (750 vs. 70,000–300,000) and oppositional in ethos. HoBB literally began as a counter-event to these mega-conferences.

**Compared to Burning Man:** the resonance is deeper — both are experiential, participatory, anti-conventional. But HoBB is squarely situated within the professional-corporate world.

**Core currency comparison:** Davos trades in power, TED in ideas, SXSW in trends, Web Summit in deals, Burning Man in experience, HoBB in meaning.

### Event history

- **2017:** Barcelona (guild house pop-up) and Lisbon (alongside Web Summit)
- **2019:** Lisbon ("The Battle for Beautiful Business," ~500 attendees, former bank)
- **2021:** Lisbon ("Concrete Love," hybrid format)
- **2023:** Sintra, Portugal ("The___Dream," 600 attendees, former Arthur Conan Doyle estate)
- **2024:** Tangier, Morocco ("Between the Two of Us," 600–750 attendees from 40+ countries, Moulay Hafid Palace)
- **2025:** Multi-city series across Berlin, New York, Tangier, Istanbul
- **2026:** Athens (World Beautiful Business Forum, 750+ expected) — the 10th anniversary

### What the Forum is not

No PowerPoints, no name tags, no panels in the conventional sense, no sales pitches. Programming unfolds through deep conversations, silent dinners, ideation walks, social experiments, live music, theatre performances, sound baths, LARPs, curated dinners, football matches, Mystery Meetings, Speed-Date a Philosopher, and Dangerous Ideas exchanges. Music, dance, and theatre are not sideshows — they are central.

## Part 2 — The World Beautiful Business Forum: Athens 2026

### Concept and frame

The Forum arrives in Athens on May 7–10, 2026, for HoBB's 10th anniversary — its most ambitious gathering yet. Athens was chosen explicitly for its heritage as "the cradle of philosophy, humanism, Europe, and democracy" and "a crossroads of East and West." Leberecht frames the city as "nostalgic and irreverent at once, complex and full of paradox, marked by a gritty, honest beauty the world urgently needs."

The frame above the frame is "the most human gathering for the more-than-human world" — an operationalisation of HoBB's "life-centered economy" into civilisational register. Six venues including the Olympia City Music Theatre "Maria Callas" (mainstage) and the former Athens Stock Exchange.

### Five-act dramaturgy

The main stage is structured as a Greek drama in five acts:

**Act One — The Story of Us** (Day 1 evening, hosted by Till and Tim). Constituting the "we."

**Act Two — The End of ~~Business~~ Humanity As We Know It** (Day 2 morning, hosted by Bruno Giussani). The strikethrough on "Business" is in the official program copy; it performs, at the level of the session title, the move from business questions to civilisational ones.

**Act Three — Clash of the Titans** (Day 2 mid, hosted by David Gelles). The formal contest.

**Act Four — How I Learned to Love Synthetic** (Day 3 morning, hosted by Till and Meryem). The reversal — a deliberate riff on *Dr. Strangelove*. Ironic acceptance rather than embrace or rejection of the synthetic condition.

**Act Five — Beastopia** (Day 3 evening, hosted by Till, Meryem, and Tim). The departure into uncharted territory — not redemption, not collapse, not a return to the human. The closing show lives here.

### Ten thematic tracks

Each track operates as a "department" of a fictional state — a coherent cosmology that frames the event as a provisional alternative polity:

| Track | Sessions | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Community | 32 | Connective tissue: dinners, tours, picnics, activations, the party |
| Bureau of Care | 27 | 1:1s, bilateral meetings, intimacy workshops, body-based sessions |
| Longevity Lab | 11 | Aging, intergenerational, psychedelics, family, ancestors |
| Home of Belonging | 8 | Surrender, belonging, identity, George Papandreou |
| Ministry of Regeneration | 8 | Post-growth, doughnut economics, regenerative exchange, Pension Poker |
| Beauty Salon | 8 | Design, aesthetics, ugliness, cocktails with artists |
| AI Democracy Marathon | 8 | More-than-human democracy, citizen assembly, nightwalks, the AIssembly |
| Agentic Agora | 8 | AI agency, agentic organisations, collective agency, context engineering |
| Temple of Reenchantment | 7 | Magic, cleaning, sacred business, soul presence |
| Department of Depth | 7 | Good life, meaning, philosophical speed-dating rave, idiots, myths |
| Main Stage | 5 | Five acts |
| Beautiful Business School | 2 | Archetypes, kitchen-together |

Plus cross-track sessions: Reality Tunnels (Agentic Agora × AI Democracy Marathon), Aegean Intelligence (Agentic Agora × Dept of Depth), Birthplace of Democracy Tour (AI Democracy Marathon × Dept of Depth).

### The AI Democracy Marathon

The signature civic element. A human-machine collaboration involving facilitated walks across Athens totalling 42.2 kilometres (the original Marathon distance) over 42.195 hours. Each walk invites "a moving dialogue on how democracy can be preserved, transformed, or reimagined in a more-than-human world." When humans rest, machines continue — the AIssembly takes the night shift. The walks connect to the Pnyx and Athens' democratic heritage. DemocracyNext is an official partner; citizen assembly formats are woven into the program.

Marathon sessions: The More-Than-Human Democracy, Human Democracy Is Dead, The Dark Side of Democracy (Nightwalk), Dance and Dissent (Nightwalk), Citizen Assembly With AI, Make Politics Great Again, Birthplace of Democracy Tour, and two instances of The AIssembly.

### 202 contributors — seven intellectual factions

The contributor list is more plural than the brand voice suggests. Reading it carefully, seven identifiable factions emerge, each with its own canon, position, and characteristic blind spot:

**Faction A — Cognitive Sovereignty Hawks.** Carissa Véliz, Bruno Giussani, Elaine Kasket, Maggie Jackson, Dave and Helen Edwards. Canon: Zuboff, Crawford, NATO cognitive warfare doctrine. Position: AI's primary threat is the erosion of autonomous thought and emotional sovereignty. Véliz's *Prophecy* publishes April 21, 2026, sixteen days before the forum; Giussani is hosting Act Two with *Our Minds Under Siege* in hand; Kasket has already publicly dismantled Hotwire AI Lab's Anol Bhattacharya on HoBB's own Substack in October 2025. Armed, present, and demonstrated willing.

**Faction B — Sovereign AI Builders.** Sean White (Inflection AI), Anol Bhattacharya (Hotwire), Gianni Giacomelli (MIT CCI), Arathi Sethumadhavan (Google), Ted Lappas (Conscium/WPP), Sam Glassenberg. Position: AI can be designed with empathy, collective intelligence, and benefit as goals — the question is implementation. Structurally in tension with Faction A, engineered to share the Act Two stage.

**Faction C — Posthumanist Reframers.** Bayo Akomolafe, James Bridle (lives on Aegina, 45 minutes from Athens), Indy Johar, Ezgi Hamzaçebi, Nicole Miller, Despina Papadopoulos. Canon: Haraway, Latour, Barad, indigenous cosmologies. Position: the category "human" is the problem, not the solution; intelligence is distributed. Akomolafe's "technologies of humiliation" frame and Bridle's critique of corporations as already-existing AI are the sharpest philosophical tools in the room.

**Faction D — Anti-Capitalist / Collapse-Aware.** Clara Mattei (*The Capital Order*, *Escape from Capitalism* January 2026), Luke Kemp (*Goliath's Curse*, Cambridge CSER), Anastasia Linn (Arketa Institute of Post-Growth Finance), Ilektra Kouloumpi (Doughnut Economics Action Lab), David Gelles (hosting Act Three). Position: the system cannot be reformed from within; build alternative institutions. Mattei's critique of capitalism-as-organised-exploitation makes the €1,895 price point a live wire.

**Faction E — Democratic Institutionalists.** Claudia Chwalisz (DemocracyNext, co-partner on the AI Democracy Marathon), Jon Alexander (New Citizenship Project, *Citizens*), Geoff Mulgan (UCL), Lisa Witter (Apolitical), Roger Berkowitz (Hannah Arendt Center), George Papandreou (former PM, General Rapporteur for Democracy at the Council of Europe), Monique van Dusseldorp (AI Democracy Marathon curator). Chwalisz's sortition-based citizens' assemblies and Alexander's citizen-vs-consumer frame are direct neighbours to what the AIssembly claims to be.

**Faction F — Re-Enchantment / Spiritual.** Tara Isabella Burton, Shoukei Matsumoto (Buddhist monk, collaborates with Markus Gabriel on AI ethics), Nikki Trott, Chris Everett, Christoph Quarch (New Platonic Academy, trained under Gadamer), Sandra Blühdorn, Maxime Rovere. Position: the crisis is one of meaning; beauty, awe, and spiritual practice are necessary responses.

**Faction G — Security / Geopolitics Realists.** Elisabeth Braw (Atlantic Council, *Goodbye, Globalization*), Sharon Dolev and Emad Kiyaei (Middle East Treaty Organization), John Katsos (business in conflict zones, 2024/2025 Nobel Peace Prize nominee). Almost no overlap with the posthumanist or re-enchantment clusters — itself a finding. The program places consciousness-and-beauty thinking next to undersea-cable-sabotage thinking.

**Plus a substantive Greek / Aegean intellectual layer.** Quarch, Nikos Erinakis (authenticity and AI, University of Crete), Stavroula Tsinorema (Greek National Bioethics Commission), Despina Papadopoulos (*AI as Philosophy Machine*, Routledge 2026), Vassilis Koutsoumpas (negotiated Greece's OpenAI education partnership), Papandreou. Not a faction — a presence. The line between substantive Greek philosophical depth and civilisational prestige-borrowing is thin. "Aegean Intelligence" functions as a civilisational counter-pitch to Silicon Valley.

### Four engineered tensions

The curators have deliberately staged specific oppositions:

- **Véliz × White on Act Two.** Prediction-as-power thesis directly adjacent to emotional-AI product.
- **Kasket × Bhattacharya in Agentic Agora.** Kasket's Substack rebuttal, brought into the same physical space.
- **Braw alongside Dolev and Kiyaei.** Defending vs. dismantling Western security order, in Athens.
- **Mattei in the Ministry of Regeneration at the former Athens Stock Exchange, alongside the Endeavor Greece startup ecosystem and Lars Rasmussen.** Post-growth economics on a Greek financial trading floor.

The curatorial move: contradictions held together *are* the content. Tim Leberecht has confessed the program has become an autonomous system exceeding his cognitive capacity to navigate.

### Center of gravity

The marketing foregrounds beauty, care, belonging, and regeneration. The program's actual concentration of intellectual energy and Main Stage placement is on **agency** — who acts, who decides, what happens when machines share or seize those functions. Beauty, care, and belonging are the experiential conditions under which the harder agency questions become askable. They are the connective tissue. Agency is what the room is actually about.

### Signature sessions

Beyond the five acts and the Marathon: Athens Regenerative Exchange (a social experiment on the trading floor of the former Stock Exchange testing alternative economic frameworks), Pension Poker (a game exposing extractive finance), Shoukei Matsumoto cleaning the venue as opening ritual, Philosophical Speed-Dating Rave, The Ugliest Dinner Party in the World, The Pressure Lab: Dream Under Fire, Six Cases of Beautiful Business, Ten Years of Beautiful Business.

### Key contributors (sample from 202)

Notable contributors include: former IDEO CEO Sandy Speicher, current IDEO CEO Mike Peng, former Google Creative Lab Executive Creative Director Steve Vranakis, Steelcase Global VP Kim Dabbs, Accor Global Brand President Benoît Racle, Mozilla Foundation VP Kyle Baptista, INSEAD professors Gianpiero and Jennifer Petriglieri, Chris Luebkeman (strategic foresight), Geoff Mulgan (social imagination, UCL), Payal Arora (Inclusive AI Lab, Utrecht), Hannah Critchlow (neuroscientist), Lars Rasmussen (co-founder Google Maps), Mark Aanderud (Musical Director and Curator for HoBB), and Meryem Lahrichi (Experience Design Lead, HoBB; host of the Forum).

## Part 3 — The Audience

### The important distinction

The contributors are not the audience. They are what the audience came to encounter. The audience profile is an inference — reading the contributor list, the brand voice, the program conventions, and the price point as a demand signal tells us who is willing to pay €1,895 to be in that specific room for four days.

### Demographics

**Professional composition.** A deliberate mix of corporate executives (C-suite and VP-level), management consultants, entrepreneurs and founders, designers and creatives, academics and researchers, artists and performers, coaches and facilitators, nonprofit leaders, and activists. Organisations represented include Google, Microsoft, SAP, Amazon, Apple, BCG, McKinsey-affiliated kyu, IDEO, frog design, Porsche, BMW, Chanel, LVMH, Deutsche Bank, HSBC, Coca-Cola, IKEA, Pfizer, Lufthansa, Greenpeace, UNICEF, and the UN. The corporate executive cohort is substantial — these are people whose companies pay the €1,895 standard ticket — but they mingle with freelancers, students (discounted passes available), and stipend recipients.

**Geography.** Predominantly European — strong German, Portuguese, Scandinavian, and broader EU contingents — with a significant North American presence and growing Global South representation, particularly since the Tangier events. 40+ countries.

**Age.** Likely centres on 35–55, mixing senior executives in their 40s and 50s with younger founders, designers, and emerging leaders.

**Education.** Very high — deeply literate, comfortable with philosophical concepts, foreign-language vocabulary (*Bildung*, *alexithymia*), and interdisciplinary thinking. Gender balance deliberately curated toward parity.

**Price range.** €395 (Greek residents) to €1,895 (standard pass), with nonprofit, student, and stipend options. The economic range is real but skews affluent.

### What the contributor list tells us about who is in the room

An audience willing to pay to be in this room is composed of people who:

- **Want to be with seven factions simultaneously.** Most professional audiences cluster around one canon — the McKinsey crowd, the Davos crowd, the Esalen crowd. This audience pays a premium *not* to cluster. They find belonging in the friction between factions.
- **Read across traditions as identity, not hobby.** Working familiarity with Arendt, doughnut economics, cognitive warfare, posthumanism — the price of admission, already paid before the ticket.
- **Trust HoBB's curation.** A Steelcase VP, an Accor brand president, and a former Greek PM all said yes to the same room. The audience inherits that trust.
- **Want permission to think across their own contradictions.** They did not come to be told they are right. They came to be told their contradictions are real.
- **Care about how the encounter is staged as much as what is staged.** Form is content.
- **Absorb seriousness without solemnity.** Professional adults who have not lost the capacity for play and actively distrust contexts that have.
- **Skew toward the integrating function in their own work.** IDEO, M13, Steelcase, Mozilla, Hotwire, Westernacher, ada Learning, SYPartners — organisations whose work is integration across disciplines and sectors. The largest cohort is professionals whose jobs require holding multiple registers simultaneously.
- **Have made a deliberate choice against Davos, SXSW, Web Summit, and Burning Man.** €1,895 is buying access to a particular kind of company — and the company is the other 749 people, more than the speakers.
- **Want to be tested, not flattered.** Accomplished enough for the price, humble enough to want four days with people who know more.

### What the brand voice tells us

The Ministry of Regeneration / Temple of Reenchantment / Department of Depth vocabulary is an audience filter. The audience is composed of people who:

- Find institutional language repurposed as utopian poetic-political gesture witty rather than precious.
- Accept spiritual vocabulary without being religious. Secular but reverent, modern but enchantable.
- Treat the playful as a marker of seriousness, not its opposite.
- Want to be part of something that has a worldview. HoBB is not neutral.
- Tolerate — and reward — institutional self-mythology. Ten-year history, the Substack, the manifesto. They like being part of something with a story.

### Relationship to technology

Neither techno-optimist nor techno-dystopian — a critical humanist position that might best be described as "post-digital." They use and even partner with AI while insisting that human agency, judgment, and meaning remain central. Deeply skeptical of Silicon Valley's "move fast" culture, algorithmic logic, and attention extraction. HoBB cut ties with X/Twitter years ago. Their Substack reports that 69% of surveyed professionals feel empowered by AI while 56% believe AI could do their job within five years. This audience holds that tension rather than resolving it.

### Values and worldview — seven core assumptions

1. **Business can and should be transformed from within** — not abolished, but made beautiful. Reformism, not revolution.
2. **The humanities and arts hold answers that business schools don't** — philosophy, poetry, and performance are functional, not decorative.
3. **Intimacy and vulnerability are professional virtues** — sharing personal stories within five minutes of meeting is celebrated.
4. **The "metacrisis" framing is shared ground** — AI acceleration, ecological collapse, and democratic erosion are existential, interconnected challenges.
5. **Experience trumps information** — embodied, felt knowledge matters more than slide decks.
6. **Global cosmopolitanism is assumed** — transnational networks and European/Mediterranean cultural capital are valued.
7. **Paradox is preferable to resolution** — complexity, contradiction, and "unsafe ideas" are welcomed; tidy frameworks are rejected.

### Synthesis — who the audience actually is

Senior professionals working in integrating functions — founders, designers, strategy people, transformation leaders, creatives, consultants. Accomplished enough to be uncomfortable with their own success. Literate enough to read across traditions. Uncluttered enough to leave the standard prestige circuits. Uncertain enough to want their assumptions tested. Playful enough to read wit as seriousness. Secular but enchantable. Willing to pay a premium for curation they trust. Looking primarily for the company of similar people rather than for content delivery. Already internally holding contradictions they don't have language for and looking to the room to articulate them.

They are romantic reformists: committed to transforming capitalism from within through beauty, meaning, and humaneness.

### Activation and failure conditions

**Activates on.** Genuine intellectual risk delivered with craft. Naming of contradictions the room holds but rarely articulates. Paradox they haven't encountered before. Felt experience over information transfer. Specific named individuals taking specific named positions. Embodied or performative elements. Material that can be held by multiple factions without flattening any of them. The unfamiliar voice that turns out to think differently from them.

**Goes flat on.** Standard progressive talking points without edge. Corporate sustainability vocabulary. Academic jargon without grounding. TED-style resolution toward inspiration. Clinical framings of spiritual material, devotional treatment of secular material. Bullet-pointed slide decks. Earnestness without irony. Cynicism without warmth. One faction's position dressed as universal truth. Anything Véliz or Giussani would dismiss as cognitive capture in literary clothing.

**Stretch — the five friction points.** They critique extraction while embedded in extractive systems. Their embrace of paradox has become comfortable. "Beautiful capitalism is still capitalism." Their cosmopolitanism carries Western philosophical centrism. Their own organisations are not democratic. Whether "more-than-human democracy" is genuine expansion or category error. **And — the deepest one for an AI-mediated experiment — their well-curated openness is itself the failure mode: they are too good at performing reception to know when they are not actually being changed.**

### What would genuinely provoke them

The sharpest provocations target the contradictions this community holds but struggles to resolve:

- **The class and privilege blind spot.** The tension between flying to Mediterranean gatherings to discuss ecological collapse. Acknowledged but unresolved.
- **The "redwashing" risk.** Some past speakers felt like "postcard picture agents of redwashing, allowing companies and brands to associate with social justice themes without fundamentally addressing extractive capitalism."
- **The echo chamber concern.** HoBB itself admits it wants to "move beyond our echo chamber." Genuine ideological collision — not just intellectual diversity within a shared progressive framework — remains limited.
- **The sufficiency of aesthetic reform.** The system's problems may be structural, not aesthetic, and "beautiful capitalism" is still capitalism.
- **European/Western philosophical centrism.** Despite engaging Global South thinkers, the institutional DNA, funding model, and intellectual references remain heavily European.

### The audience's deepest vulnerability

They have built an identity around holding paradox, embracing complexity, and welcoming discomfort — which means the thing that would most genuinely unsettle them is the suggestion that their embrace of paradox has itself become comfortable, and that their aesthetic of discomfort has become its own form of complacency. As Giridharadas — speaking at their own event — put it: they may be better at dismantling systems than building new ones.

### What this audience means for the AI Assembly specifically

This is the most sympathetic possible audience for the provotype. They are not hostile to the premise. They chose to attend an event called the "World Beautiful Business Forum" and many will participate in a track called the "AI Democracy Marathon." This matters for what the provotype can claim: whatever happens in Athens cannot be generalised to a city council, a parliament, or a planning commission. But it can test whether the provotype itself works — whether anything happens at all to a conversation when non-humans are part of it. If it doesn't work even here, the concept has a problem.

They are capable of producing Layer 3 expansion and equally capable of *performing* expansion without being changed by it. The provotype is designed to surface which is happening.

The architectural consequence: the Assembly has to be designed against the audience's hospitality instinct. The artifacts that succeed will be the ones the audience keeps returning to not because they were beautiful but because they were unresolved.
