# Nine Modes of Implication

*Reference for the AI Assembly media system. The goal of every surface is to turn viewers into participants before the content opens. Implication ≠ participation: a poll that tallies and moves on is participatory but rarely implicating. A mode implicates when the viewer's own position becomes the ground they read the rest of the content on.*

---

## The nine modes

**1. Comparing.** Two positions, pick which is closer to yours, *before* reading further. Kills it: a neutral/both option. The forced binary is the implicating force.

**2. Locating.** Plot yourself on the matrix before the voices arrive. Kills it: letting the viewer move their dot after seeing the voices. Commit first, see second.

**3. Predicting.** Guess what the voice said before reading. Kills it: predictions too hard or too easy. The sweet spot — confident guess, subtly wrong answer.

**4. Ranking.** Order the twelve voices on a specific dimension (most alien, most disagreeable, most persuasive) before entering. Kills it: flat affective criteria like "favourite."

**5. Annotating.** Private margin notes that persist across visits. Kills it: social annotation. Public marks convert reckoning into performance.

**6. Choosing what to see.** First draft or amended. Prose or document form. Kills it: more than two options. Choice fatigue collapses to hospitality.

**7. Returning with context.** Day 2 additions feel like gifts, not assignments. The archive rewards fidelity; yesterday's reading becomes richer today. Kills it: making return feel like homework.

**8. Deciding on behalf of.** The viewer as silent juror — which voice should carry on this theme. Kills it: making the judgement feel consequential beyond the page. Keep it as private verdict.

**9. Completing.** Artifacts end on open questions. Quadrants show visible gaps labelled *no voice went here*. Kills it: incompleteness that looks like error rather than intention.

---

## Surface-fit (rough cut)

| Surface | Strongest modes |
|---|---|
| Micro-site | Locating, predicting, choosing, returning |
| Substack | Comparing, predicting |
| Closing show | Deciding, completing |
| Daily newsletter blurb | Comparing |

## CTAs

Generic CTAs (*read more*, *share*) are hospitality fuel. The CTAs that work require commitment *before* the content opens.

Not: *read the Octopus's position →*

But: *choose a side. Then read the Octopus's position →*

Same click. The second requires the viewer to have committed first. The click becomes the implication.

---

## Polling — the trap and the unlock

**Weak polling (hospitality fuel):** aggregate results shown. *71% agreed with the Octopus.* The crowd becomes the frame. Viewer locates themselves in the majority or the interesting dissent. Encounter metabolised.

**Strong polling unlocks:**

- **Result withheld.** Viewer commits without seeing what others chose. No crowd-as-cover.
- **Result reframes content.** Poll first, artifact read against the viewer's own answer — not against the crowd.
- **Over time, not over people.** Poll Day 1, read, poll Day 3. *You chose A. Now you chose B. What shifted?* Self-drift is more implicating than crowd-position.

---

## The underlying principle

**The viewer must do something before the content opens, and what they did has to matter for how they read.**

Commitment-then-content → implicating.
Content-then-commitment → consumptive.

Most web design defaults to content-first-then-action-after. This project defaults to the inverse.
