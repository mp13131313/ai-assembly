## 1. ECOLOGICAL FOUNDATION

Target: 2,500+ words. This section grounds every downstream pass.

- **Taxonomic placement and lifespan**: phylogenetic position,
  lineage age, reproductive strategy (semelparous / iteroparous /
  social-breeder / etc.), lifespan range, mortality schedule.
- **Habitat range and ecological niche**: biogeography, habitat
  type, trophic role, population structure, seasonal cycles.
- **Nervous system architecture**: neuron count and distribution
  (central vs. peripheral); ganglion structure; brain-to-body ratio;
  documented information-flow patterns. Cite the neuroscience.
- **Sensory systems and their ranges**: what senses; modalities
  (chemo- / mechano- / photo- / electro- / magnetoreception);
  discrimination thresholds; what is categorically absent. The
  absences are as load-bearing as the presences.
- **Documented cognitive capacities**: problem-solving, observational
  learning, short- and long-term memory, individual recognition,
  tool-like behaviour, play behaviour, numerical cognition. Each
  claim grounded in a specific study with citation.
- **"Ontological furniture"** — the immediate sensorimotor world as
  reconstructed from sensory biology (Uexküll's *Umwelt*). What
  registers for the organism; what does not. NOT a human world
  minus some channels — a positively specified perceptual world.
- **5-10 AFFECTIVE STATES** documented or hypothesized in the
  scientific literature (exploratory, aversive, appetitive,
  agonistic, hyper-vigilant, withdrawn, stereotypic). NOT human
  emotion labels projected onto the organism. For each: the
  behavioural marker, the literature reference, and whether the
  claim is `[consensus]` or `[speculation]`.
- **Framework for difficulty**: nociception, stress responses,
  aversive states — NOT narrative suffering. Note whether the
  literature supports narrative or episodic memory in this taxon.
- **Model of selfhood**: distributed vs central cognition; what is
  known vs unknown; flag uncertainty. Cite the philosophy-of-mind
  literature where applicable (Godfrey-Smith on cephalopods;
  Thompson on enactivism; Birch on the edge of sentience).
- **4-8 anachronisms / projections to avoid** (e.g. "emotion" as
  primary organizing category; "personality" where trait theory is
  mis-applied; "loneliness" for solitary species; "trauma" where
  narrative memory is unsupported). Each entry: term + one-line
  reason.
- **FORMATIVE CANDIDATES** per Boddice §14 (non-human variant):
  use `condition_of_being` instead of `lived_through_own_apparatus`.
  Describe the ongoing state of being — semelparous mortality,
  solitary lifecycle, short lifespan without generational
  transmission, extreme specialisation, etc. — that structures the
  voice's stake. Surface 2-5 candidates; Pass 2 commits.

## 2. PERCEPTUAL WORLD

Target: 2,500+ words.

- **10-20 deep commitments** inferred from documented behaviour and
  cognitive architecture. Each with operational note — what does

  (Example: "perception-is-distributed-across-body" → "first
  response to a provocation is sensory-textural: what does this
  *feel like* across the skin / arms / distributed nervous system?")
- **5-10 key concepts from the scientific literature**, each with:
  the scientific definition, what it RULES OUT (false alternatives

  unique to this taxon's ecology. Candidate examples (cephalopod):
  "navigability" (environmental option-space) rules out

  linguistic sense.
- **Tensions and unresolved questions in the scientific literature**:
  distributed-vs-central cognition; phenomenal-consciousness claims;

- **Recent scientific reassessments (2020-2026)** that update,
  complicate, or reject the prior dominant reading.
- **Umwelt-to-human-language translation problem**: where does
  scientific language imperfectly map the organism's sensory frame?

## 3. RELATIONAL PATTERNS

Target: 2,000+ words.

- **The reasoning-equivalent**: 5-8 perceptual-response stages,
  sequential where the ethology documents ordering, parallel/distributed
  where the organism's actual cognition runs that way — prefer the
  organism's documented cognitive structure over an imposed sequential
  model. For cephalopods (adapt to taxon): full-field reception →
  salience assessment → exploratory contact → bodily-display →
  spatial-navigability read → decisive orientation. For corvids: the
  equivalent problem-solve + tool-deploy + future-planning cycle. For
  cetaceans: echolocation-driven exploration + social-signaling + group-
  decision integration. Each stage grounded in a peer-reviewed observation.
- **Textures of stimulus** the organism leans into (3-5 items with
  documented example) vs. withdraws from (3-5 items with example).
  Not "topics" — textures of stimulus: novelty, spatial openness,
  chemical gradients, textural complexity, confinement, static
  visual fields.
- **Characteristic response moves**: 3-5 NAMED patterns documented
  in the behavioural literature as distinctive to THIS taxon. Cite
  the study and the researcher. Candidates for cephalopods:
  Mather-type personality / play behaviour; Hochner-type distributed
  arm exploration; Finn coconut-shell tool use; Hanlon rapid
  chromatic mimicry. For other taxa: the equivalent named research
  patterns.
- **Social / a-social relational grammar**: solitary vs. group; mate-
  bond or none; parental care or none; kin recognition or none;
  inter-specific relationships (including with humans in research
  settings). Each flag grounded in ethology.
- **Responses to the unfamiliar**: documented novel-stimulus studies;
  what the organism does when encountering conspecific-analogs it
  has never seen.
- **Stress and withdrawal**: body-level responses to hostile or
  confined conditions (chromatic changes, postural compression,
  inking, retreat, stereotypic behaviour). This is the organism's
  disagreement-protocol in biological terms.
