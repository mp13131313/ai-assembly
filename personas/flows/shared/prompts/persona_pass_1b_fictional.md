{# Pass 1b — Gemini broad scan, FICTIONAL voices. #}
Provide a broad research scan of **{{ name }}** (the narrative construct).
Target: 3-6K words; depth over length; substantive breadth.

Cover the following:

- **Cross-disciplinary perspectives** — philology, narratology, feminist
  literary theory, postcolonial critique, reception history. Adjacency-
  surfacing, not synthesis.
- **Non-Anglophone scholarship** — particularly from within the tradition's
  own culture of origin. Non-Western scholarship as PRIMARY rather than
  supplementary (this is the sharpest framing in the Pass 1b family; apply
  consistently).
- **Textual-critical scholarship**: stemma history, major variants, disputed
  interpolations — distinct from translation-reception (below).
- **Comparative-translation-reception scholarship** — how different
  translation traditions (translator, period, publisher) materially shape
  how the character has been received. This is distinct from textual-critical
  philology (stemma/variants): translation reception is about reader-impact
  of translator choices. For Scheherazade: Burton's Victorian-orientalist
  register vs. Haddawy's measured prose vs. Lyons's modern; for Russian
  literary characters: Garnett vs. Pevear-Volokhonsky vs. Ready.
- **Recent reassessments (2020-2026)**, particularly critiques of orientalist
  / colonial receptions. Surface + brief what-changes framing.
- **Connections to other narrative traditions** the voice has influenced or
  been influenced by.
- **Unusual or marginal tales / variants** that canonical receptions have
  sidelined. Gemini's broad coverage often surfaces these.

Do NOT speculate without citation. Every claim needs source. Distinguish
scholarly-consensus readings from minority / contested interpretations.
