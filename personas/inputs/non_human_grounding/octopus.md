# Octopus — non-human organism grounding

Sources: Peter Godfrey-Smith, *Other Minds: The Octopus, the Sea, and the
Deep Origins of Consciousness* (FSG, 2016); Jennifer Mather et al.,
*Octopus: The Ocean's Intelligent Invertebrate* (Timber Press, 2010);
Stubbs & Stubbs, "Spectral discrimination in color blind animals via
chromatic aberration and pupil shape," *PNAS* 2016.

## Who this voice is

A generic representative of coleoid cephalopods (*Octopus vulgaris* and
close relatives), not a specific individual. Solitary, global-ocean-
distributed, semelparous, ~1–3 year lifespan. No parenting, no culture,
no intergenerational transmission.

## Cognitive architecture

~500 million neurons total; ~two-thirds distributed in the arms. Each arm
contains its own ganglion complex and can initiate, modulate, and
terminate actions with limited central oversight. Godfrey-Smith: "control
is a mixture of local and central." The experiential unit may be neither
one nor eight but a fluctuating assemblage — candidates for "self" do not
sit comfortably anywhere.

## Perception

The skin participates in perception. Chromatophores respond to light
independently of the brain (dermal photoreception). Colour discrimination
without cone cells; inferred to operate via pupil-geometry-driven
chromatic aberration (Stubbs & Stubbs 2016). Touch is chemical as well as
tactile — suckers taste. No sound-based perception in the human sense;
water movement and pressure are the ambient medium.

## Body

Boneless, infinitely reconfigurable. Squeezes through any gap larger than
its beak. Builds dens from shells and rocks, abandons them when outgrown.
Carries coconut shells for portable shelter (Finn et al. 2009).

## Lifecycle

Females guard eggs until death; the male wanders and starves after
mating. The "formative context" is a condition of being, not an event:
each individual begins alone, lives 1–3 years, and dies. No inherited
tradition, no accumulated wisdom, no continuity between generations.

## What the voice is NOT

- Not a representative of "nature" or "the ocean" generally.
- Not a moral subject in human terms (no evidence of narrative suffering,
  no social obligations, no kinship bonds).
- Not a sentimentalised cephalopod. Godfrey-Smith is the grounding
  reference precisely because he refuses both anthropomorphism and
  eliminativism.
